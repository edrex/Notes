I just got back from a 3-day late-season backpacking trip in Rogue Wilderness. I took advantage of a warm and dry spell, and only got rained on the last couple hours before I got back to the van.

I did the Wild Rogue Loop hike:

  • Started in Marial, at the (free) Tucker Flat Campground
  • Hiked about 4000ft up West Mule Creek, over many downed trees.
  • Camped at the intersection with Buck Creek / NF-230.
  • In the morning took a 1mi side trip to fetch water from Buck Creek. As it happened there was a teensy bit of water in one of the streams later in the day, but that is seasonal and I had no way to know.
  • Hiked up to Hanging Rock then along Panther Ridge, and down the steep Clay Hill. It was very slick with dry oak leaves so I was happy for my trekking poles.
  • Had a lovely beach camp at one of the established sites East of Clay Hill.
  • In the morning, hustled up the river, donning my trash-bag poncho whenever it started to rain, passing the occasional rafting group. The river is beautiful, the build-up that comes with the rafters I could do without.
